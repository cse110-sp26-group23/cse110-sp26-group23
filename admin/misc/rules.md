# Team 23 Charter

## Team Information
- **Team Number:** 23
- **Team Name:** LeetCode James
- **Course:** CSE 110
- **Quarter:** Spring 2026

---

## 1. Team Purpose and Objectives

The purpose of our team is to work together to complete the CSE 110 project and all related assignments in a responsible and organized way. We also want to build a team environment where people communicate clearly, do their part, and support each other when deadlines get busy.

Our main goals are:
- Finish assignments and project tasks on time
- Keep communication clear and active
- Make sure everyone contributes
- Stay organized as a team
- Build good teamwork habits during the quarter

---

## 2. Team Mission

Our team’s mission is to work together in a way that is reliable, respectful, and productive. We want to make sure everyone understands what is going on, knows what they are responsible for, and feels comfortable speaking up if there is a problem.

---

## 3. Team Values and Standards

As a team, we want to follow these values:

### Respect
Listen actively, value all ideas, and be on time.

### Accountability
Everyone should be responsible for their own tasks and be honest if they are falling behind.

### Communication
We want to avoid confusion by communicating early and clearly, especially when deadlines are close.

### Reliability
If someone says they will do something, the team should be able to count on that.

### Collaboration
We want to help each other when needed and work as a team instead of everyone doing things separately with no communication.

### Flexibility
We know people have different schedules and outside responsibilities, so we want to be understanding while still being fair.

---

## 4. Common Goals

Some goals we have as a group are:
- Stay on top of deadlines
- Divide work in a fair way
- Keep our repo and documents organized
- Avoid last-minute confusion as much as possible
- Build something meaningful by the end of the quarter

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

We do not expect every role to stay fixed for the whole quarter, but we do want responsibilities to be clear.

### Team Leaders
- Help organize meetings and deadlines
- Keep the team updated on what needs to get done
- Help the group stay on track
- Step in when coordination is needed

### Meeting Notes / Documentation
- Take notes during meetings
- Keep notes and important documents in the repo

### Repository / Workflow Support
- Help keep the repo organized
- Help with pushing, branches, and making sure files are where they need to be

### All Team Members
Every member is expected to:
- Attend meetings when possible
- Complete assigned work
- Respond to important messages
- Let the team know early if something is wrong
- Stay involved in team decisions and progress

Note: Roles can change later if needed depending on workload and deadlines.

---

## 6. Accountability Structure

To keep things fair and clear:
- Tasks should be assigned clearly
- Deadlines should be communicated to everyone
- People should update the team if they are stuck or delayed
- Important decisions should be written down somewhere, usually in meeting notes or the group chat

We will mainly judge accountability by whether people are doing their work, responding when needed, and staying involved.

---

## 7. Communication Protocols

### Primary Communication
Our main communication will happen in the group chat. Important updates should be sent there so everyone can see them.

### Expected Response Time
We understand people are busy, but members should try to respond in a reasonable amount of time, especially for messages about deadlines, meetings, or urgent team decisions.

### Meeting Communication
If someone cannot attend a meeting, they should let the group know if possible and check the meeting notes later.

---

## 8. Meeting Expectations

We expect to meet at least once a week, and more often if deadlines are close or we need to figure something out quickly.

In general, meetings should include:
- what we need to get done
- what deadlines are coming up
- who is doing what
- any problems or open questions
- next steps

Meeting notes should be taken and saved in the repo.

---

## 9. Work Expectations

If someone knows they cannot finish something on time, they should say so early instead of waiting until the last minute. We also expect people to ask questions when they are confused instead of disappearing. 

---

## 10. Participation Expectations

Everyone should stay involved in the group. That includes attending meetings when possible, responding to important messages, keeping up with team updates, and helping with assignments or project tasks.


---

## 11. Conflict Resolution Strategy

### Step 1: Direct Communication
The people involved should first try to talk about the issue clearly.

### Step 2: Team Discussion
If the problem does not get resolved, the team leaders or the full group can step in and help talk it through.

### Step 3: Written Clarification
If needed, the team can restate responsibilities or expectations clearly in writing so there is less confusion.

### Step 4: TA / Mentor Support
If the issue keeps going or becomes more serious, we can bring it to the TA or mentor.

---

## 12. If Team Rules Are Not Being Followed

### First Time
We will remind the person about the expectation.

### If It Keeps Happening
We will talk about it more directly as a team and make expectations clearer.

### Continued Problems
If someone keeps missing work, ignoring communication, or not participating, the issue may need to be documented and brought to the TA or mentor.

Some examples include:
- not replying to important messages
- missing meetings with no communication
- not finishing assigned work
- not turning in something that affects the team

---

## 13. Strengths, Weaknesses, and Growth

Everyone brings different strengths to the team, and not everyone is going to be equally confident in every area. We want to make use of people’s strengths while also helping each other improve in weaker areas. If someone is less familiar with a task, the team should support them instead of just assuming they already know how to do it.

---

## 14. Success Measures

We will consider our team successful if:
- we stay reasonably on top of deadlines
- people communicate and stay involved
- our repo and documents stay organized
- we finish the quarter having worked well as a team

---

## 15. Review and Revision Policy

This charter is not finalized. If something is not working, we can come back and update it later as a group.

---

## 16. Agreement

By signing this charter, each team member agrees to make a real effort to follow these rules, communicate honestly, and contribute to the team.
